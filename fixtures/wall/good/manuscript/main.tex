\section{Methods}
We used a leave-one-subject-out protocol together with a strong Transformer baseline, and we injected synthetic jitter and channel dropout in a controlled sweep to map the exact failure thresholds of the classifier under increasing perturbation, holding all other training hyperparameters fixed across every fold of the evaluation.
\section{Results}
The measured clean accuracy was 0.71, and it degraded to 0.62 under moderate jitter, with the sharpest drop occurring once channel dropout exceeded a quarter of the montage, a pattern that held consistently across every held-out subject in the leave-one-subject-out evaluation folds we examined in this study.
