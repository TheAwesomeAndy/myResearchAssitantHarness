# Narrative policy

Framing rules that keep a paper legible and publishable.

- One idea: the paper delivers a single reusable idea and passes the
  declaration test ("The main idea of this paper is ...").
- Compound gap: the introduction ends with a gap combining at least two
  archetypes (`intro.gap`).
- Crunchy contributions: no roadmap paragraph; an itemized list of
  refutable, forward-referenced claims (`intro.roadmap`,
  `intro.contributions`, `intro.forward-refs`).
- Mole hills: define a specific, soluble problem; do not promise to solve
  a whole field.
- So-what test (Wardle's law): every claim answers "is it true?" and "who
  cares?".
- Generous related work: synthesize and credit, never enumerate or attack
  (`related.*`).
